On the pleasure of reading aloud — Manguel
The voice that reads aloud to itself is the most ancient of literary instruments. Long before the printed page, long before the silent eye learned its trick of swallowing meaning in a glance, there was the mouth, the breath, the unhurried turning of one syllable into the next. To listen is to be carried — to surrender the self briefly, the way a small boat surrenders to a current it cannot see beneath it. The voice that reads is not a recitation but an offering; it gives the text a body, a tempo, a place to live.
